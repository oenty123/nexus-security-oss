release the source code of any product or network service built on Nexus.

If you want to use Nexus in a **closed-source** product or service — without the
AGPL's source-disclosure obligations — a separate commercial license is available.

## When you need a commercial license

- You embed Nexus in a proprietary product you distribute.
- You offer Nexus (or a derivative) as a hosted/SaaS service and don't want to
  open-source your service.
- Your company policy forbids AGPL dependencies.

## When you DON'T

- Personal use, research, internal tools you don't distribute.
- Open-source projects that are themselves AGPL-compatible.
- Just running the CLI or editor extension on your own code.
